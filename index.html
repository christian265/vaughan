Interesting 3 Facts about Oman

Oman is a fascinating country with a rich culture and history. Here are three facts about Oman that you may not know:

Oman’s National Animal Is the Arabian Oryx

Oman’s national animal is the Arabian oryx, which is also the national animal of Qatar and the United Arab Emirates. The Arabian oryx is a species of antelope that was once widespread across the Arabian Peninsula. However, due to overhunting and habitat loss, the species became extinct in the wild in the 1970s. thankfully, a captive breeding program was successful in reintroducing the Arabian oryx back into its natural habitat in Oman. Today, there are an estimated 500 Arabian oryx living in the wild in Oman.

Oman Has Four UNESCO World Heritage Sites

-The Bahla Fort is a 13th-century citadel located in the town of Bahla in Oman. The fort was built by the Banu Nebhan, a tribal group who ruled over the area at the time.

-The Bat, Al Khutm and Al Achlaf Archaeological Sites are three ancient sites located in the northern part of Oman. The sites feature a number of unique archaeological features, including tombs, temples and settlements.

-The Land of Frankincense is an archaeological site located in southern Oman. The site includes a number of ancient frankincense trees, as well as a number of ruins from an ancient trading port.

-The Wadi Darbat Nature Reserve is a nature reserve located in central Oman. The reserve is home to a number of rare and endangered animals, including the Arabian leopard and the Barbary sheep.

Oman Is One of the Biggest Oil Exporters

Despite being a relatively small country, Oman is one of the world’s leading oil exporters. In 2016, the country exported around $46.4 billion worth of oil, making it the 42nd largest exporter in the world. The majority of Oman’s oil reserves are located in the southwest of the country, in an area known as the Rub’ al Khali or “Empty Quarter.”

Oman is also home to some of the world’s largest natural gas reserves. In fact, it is estimated that Oman has enough natural gas to meet its own needs for centuries. The country is currently working on plans to export this valuable resource to other countries in the region.

In addition to oil and gas, Oman is also rich in minerals such as copper, chromite, and zinc. These resources have helped to diversify the economy and reduce dependence on oil exports.
